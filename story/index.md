    <summary>Into the Dust Plain</summary>
    <div class="panel-body">
      <p>The first steps of your journey have begun.</p>
      <p>You loaded up on food (or stole it, in Elliot's case), chose your mounts (or decided you were above such things, in Aisha's case), and made your way out of the Vastwood. Still, even early on, there was something a little strange about the caravan. Hiring you at all felt like a questionable decision—especially after interactions with Finkle, and Aezos pushing Tenzoe into the campfire.</p>
      <p>Most of you were cagey, deflecting questions about your pasts or your intentions, but Bartholomew didn't seem to mind. After all, you'd only just begun traveling together.</p>
      <p>After resolving a wagon mishap with Gia's trusty carpentry tools, a dust storm of huge proportions rolled in. Rational people would have hunkered down, tied off the cargo, and protected their eyes.</p>
      <p>Finkle, however, is not a rational person.</p>
      <p>Abandoning his clothes entirely, he rode bare-assed into the Red Wind, charging headlong into the whipping, sand-choked gale. The commotion successfully drew the scavenger wargs away from the draft animals—but left the poor, incredibly stupid dwarf alone with a pack of hungry canines.</p>
      <p>Leaping into action, the rest of you rushed to defend your tiny, dense companion. With blade and arrow, you cut down the wargs, and for a moment the battle seemed to be going your way—until several slipped through the buffeting winds and closed in on Aezos.</p>
      <p>While Ezit and Elliot cleaned up the southern flank, the others moved north to intercept the fledgling sorcerer. Before he could call upon the power of his magical blood, the scavenger wargs struck, tearing deep, vicious wounds into his arms. Lifeblood spilled into the sand as the creatures crept closer, jaws snapping for the killing blow.</p>
      <p>Without a moment to spare, Aisha materialized into the fray, realizing the party was likely doomed without her intervention. She hauled Aezos to safety, taking a savage bite herself in the process, but managed to pull the fallen mage clear. Gia repaid the wargs' aggression tenfold—bringing her axe down in a mighty arc and severing the head of one of the murderous mutts.</p>
      <p>The storm still rages, and the battle is not yet over. The remaining animals are desperate and starving, willing to fight to the end with terrifying ferocity.</p>
      <p>Will the party escape the Red Wind and the scavenger wargs?<br>Will Aezos receive healing before his wounds claim his life?<br>And is the caravan—and Bartholomew—everything it seems?</p>
    </div>

  <details class="panel">
    <summary>A Contract and A Warning</summary>
    <div class="panel-body">
      <p>With blade and arrow, you lay the beasts low.</p>
      <p>Slicing and stabbing through muscle and sinew, the scavenger wargs are no match for the party. The last survivor attempts to flee, only to be smashed into a meaty pulp by Finkle, who uses the corpse of another warg as an improvised bludgeon. Aezos, however, is still a casualty. Pale, unresponsive, and injured, you rush him back to the caravan, where Jeremy (the young halfling greenhorn you encountered previously) has set up a makeshift medical tent. The caravan needs to get moving soon, but the sorcerer is in no shape to face the rigors of the road. Luckily, Jeremy knows of an oasis nearby that might have a healing herb—desert yarrow.</p>
      <p>Before the group can head out, a cloud of arrows lands throughout the camp. They slam into the soft sand, but no one is hurt. This is a warning, not an attack. Attached to one of the arrows is a missive—though only Ezit notices it. None of the rest of the party knows what it says, but judging by Ezit's stormy expression, it's nothing good. Harried on by this mysterious presence, the group makes its way to the oasis. Unfortunately, you're not the only visitor to this patch of life in the desert. A group of wasteland bandits, dressed in black leather and masks, are attempting to enter a sandstone temple, half-buried in the dunes near the water.</p>
      <p>Eschewing stealth (and caution), Tenzoe attempts to reason with the marauders, asking only to pick some desert yarrow and leave. That almost works—until Elliot hears the bandit leader mention valuables within the temple. Stepping softly between the shadows of cacti and Joshua trees, he works his way behind the head raider and puts a knife to his throat. The man quickly comes to the conclusion that the plunder isn't worth his life, and the gang scatters.</p>
      <p>Now it's all yours—if you can get inside.</p>
      <p>A gargoyle on the stone door leers above a basin that blocks the entrance. Dried brown stains mark the bowl, and it doesn't take much investigation to realize the stonework wants blood. Tenzoe offers his own. Now that he's closer to the ruins, he hears something he never thought he'd hear again—a song from his world, sung by a woman inside the ruins.</p>
      <p>The party presses inside and finds the mysterious singer. She is an incredibly beautiful blonde woman, taking notes and studying the ancient carvings. Her electric-blue eyes lock on Tenzoe, and she offers him a deal: she will provide power and guidance, and Tenzoe will be her eyes and ears within the Dust Plains, searching for information regarding "He Who Slumbers Beneath the Sands." After signing the contract, the woman—Beatrice—reveals herself to be a cambion of the Ashen Choir, and effortlessly stops attacks from Finkle and Aisha. Unperturbed, Beatrice gives Tenzoe a notebook and directs the group to travel further into the ruins.</p>
      <p>Gia and Elliot solve the first puzzle after just a moment of thought, deftly activating pressure plates in the correct order and causing the sandstone walls to give way. This leads to the second trial—a test more intimate, personal, and simple. It may be that Finkle is even simpler, though.</p>
      <p>The statue at the center of the room—an intricately carved elven woman, hand cupped to her ear—is the source of a powerful, sound-stifling magic. It is not, unfortunately, axe-proof. The first swing sends the party flying with a blast of force, but Finkle decides to double down (much to everyone else's frustration). The second mighty blow ruptures the ley lines forming the statue's enchantment and causes the statue itself to explode. This clears the way to the final chamber, but the resultant noise wakes creatures that live within the dark cracks of the temple.</p>
      <p>Sand scorpions, the size of dogs, crawl from the crevices, but they are no match for your party. Everyone is getting sharper and more dangerous as they survive the Dust Plains—especially Tenzoe, who blasts the bugs apart with his new eldritch power. Once the battle is over, Finkle digs through the wreckage of the statue and finds a shimmering golden band. Upon putting on the ring, Finkle is magically disguised as an attractive, stern-faced githyanki woman. To everyone's amusement, this is the Ring of Shifting Fate, which magically disguises the wearer as a random race, and always as a beautiful woman of that race.</p>
      <p>The final chamber is a cramped, windowless room, where a large sarcophagus is being interred. The coffin is heavy stone, and surrounded by desert yarrow. As Elliot goes to prepare his trap-disarming tools, Finkle rips the lid off the sarcophagus. It is empty, save for a magical weapon and a few trinkets. Elliot's instincts are right, however, and the violent entry causes another trap to trigger—a deadly one. Sand pours through cracks in the walls and holes in the ceiling. The entire temple begins sinking, being reclaimed by the Dust Plains.</p>
      <p>Aisha moves first, sensing opportunity—she swipes the loot and teleports away. Gia and Ezit grab what yarrow they can carry (and their tiny dwarf companion) and rush to follow. Tenzoe tries to slow or stop the collapse with his newfound abilities, but it's simply too much. He might've perished in that sandtrap if Ezit hadn't returned to help pull him out of a sandpit. The party manages to escape as the temple sinks into the dunes behind them.</p>
      <p>Divvying up the loot and almost getting cursed for life, the party heads back relatively unscathed, with plenty of herbs for Aezos. Vesuya is just on the horizon—and with it, a brand-new brace of questions.</p>
      <p>What does Beatrice want with the sleeper beneath the dunes?<br>Will Ezit suffer the consequences of her past?<br>What will your party spend their pay on?</p>

  <details class="panel">
    <summary>Paint the Town</summary>
    <div class="panel-body">
      <p>With the desert yarrow in hand, your group made its way back to the caravan, where Jeremy tended Aezos' wounds inside the medical tent. Some of you helped clean injuries and replace sutures; others prepared your mounts for the last leg of the journey. Before long, the wounded sorcerer was back on his feet.</p>
      <p>Moving on in the early hours of morning, the party climbed a massive sand dune. At the peak, Vesuva unfurled below you: a lawless, dusty frontier town, full of intrigue and danger in equal measure. It was a ramshackle place built up after the discovery of a lucrative silver mine—the source of most of the town's livelihood. Bartholomew was pleased with the caravan's progress and paid each of you a meager wage in gold for your help. He also promised a bonus if you would meet him at the ranch south of town after supper. Until then, he encouraged you to enjoy the benefits of civilization: booze, bartering, and brothels.</p>
      <p>With pockets full of gold, most of your party went shopping and met a squat, ruddy halfling named Gideon. Gideon was a shady character, but that didn't stop you from purchasing goods. Aezos even convinced him to open his "special inventory"—in exchange for a favor, of course. The town doctor had been giving away food and medicine to the less fortunate, cutting into Gideon's profits. She needed to be… convinced… that her reckless generosity was going to get her hurt.</p>
      <p>It was a distasteful deal, and the group immediately decided to con the conman. Despite a rocky introduction—courtesy of Finkle and his goat—Dr. Silvra was willing to help, even agreeing to play along. She provided proof, too: a severed finger from a patient whose wounds had been too severe (morbid, but convincing). In return, she asked for a little investigative work: find out who had been digging up bodies from the graveyard.</p>
      <p>The scam worked. The unscrupulous shopkeep was glad to hand over payment and a few select goods. Aisha, however, noticed something strange: the rooms were marked with sigils and runes meant to ward away the undead.</p>
      <p>While the party schemed and spent, Ezit headed straight to the saloon. They had a thirst for more information—and a thirst for whiskey. Bartenders are usually excellent sources of gossip, and the harengon ranger got an earful. The town was spooked by a spate of grave robberies and the appearance of a new gang: the Warren Kings. These rough riders were harengon as well, but tougher and meaner than any seen outside the Dust Plains. Ezit knocked back a few drinks until they saw the party heading toward the graveyard and decided to tag along. There were quite a few people in the bar eyeing them with intense, unfriendly interest, after all.</p>
      <p>The graveyard was a lonely, neglected place, surrounded by a wrought-iron fence and scraggly desert vegetation creeping around crumbling headstones. As you approached, the air turned cold—an unnatural chill that defied the midday sun. A man paced among the graves, muttering over a leather-bound book. He was a drow, and he was clearly performing some profane magic on the recently dead.</p>
      <p>Your group startled him, and he explained that he'd been paid to be there—paid by Caldera Mining Company to find a new source of labor. Silver mining is dangerous work, and refining the ore involves molten, toxic chemicals. It was the sort of place where workers would demand things like "rest," "safety," and "pay"—all of which cut into the company's profits. An undead worker would have none of these qualms.</p>
      <p>Aezos was having none of it, offended by the necromancer's brazen affront to decency. He attempted a daring maneuver, blasting the drow's book with a gust of air. If everything went to plan, Aisha would catch it. Everything did not, in fact, go according to plan.</p>
      <p>The summoner not only kept hold of his tome—he called up a pack of the living dead to devour the party. Finkle and Tenzoe leapt into melee, smiting down the dusty dead, only to be blasted back by a wave of eldritch energy. Elliot and Aisha focused on the necromancer, landing two blows in quick succession: a throwing dagger pierced his shoulder, and an instant later an arrow punctured his eye. The drow fell, and without their master's will holding the rotting flesh together, the undead collapsed back into the earth.</p>
      <p>Searching the ritual site revealed a number of disturbing truths. First, several of the parts the necromancer had been using were fresh—too fresh to be remains from dug-up cadavers. Second, the man was a warlock, much like Tenzoe. Studying his grimoire, Tenzoe learned the dead man's pact mirrored his own: a bargain forged in the search for He Who Sleeps Beneath the Sands. Finally, the party learned that the drow had a meeting scheduled with the Warren Kings at the bathhouse. There might have been even more to learn, if Finkle hadn't decided to destroy the evil artifact by eating it. Tenzoe decided the best way to uncover more was to impersonate the drow and attend the meetup himself.</p>
      <p>Gia split off as the party made its way to the Mahogany Staircase, the brothel and bathhouse on Vesuva's main street. She wanted to meet the local color—and catch a few drinks herself. Instead of a raucous saloon full of music and dancing, she found a stage missing its star. "Andre the Amazing" had vanished, and the crowd was getting restless. The staff was desperate for performers, willing to offer the spotlight to just about anyone. Even an eleven-foot barbarian lizard, it turns out.</p>
      <p>And much to their surprise, Gia wasn't just a scaly axe-murder machine. Her dulcet, sultry voice—and a touching song about working for a living—brought the house down. The grateful owner offered complimentary food and lodging in Andre's old room. The room was a mess; whatever happened to Andre had clearly involved a struggle.</p>
      <p>At the bathhouse, Tenzoe walked straight through the front door while the others set themselves up in tactical positions. Well—except Ezit, who opted for a very tactical rosewater bath. Aisha climbed to the second-floor balcony while Elliot listened at the meeting room door.</p>
      <p>Whatever Tenzoe expected to find up there, a captive aasimar was not on the list. A pair of Warren Kings were holding the performer hostage, planning to sell off his wings. Their divine nature would have made an excellent fuel source for dark rituals. Tenzoe managed to intimidate the gangsters into releasing their prey. Elliot and Aisha tailed the duo as they left—unhappy, but preoccupied. It seemed they'd found someone who could be even more valuable…</p>
      <p>The group reconvened at the saloon, where Gia had secured lodging and food. They discussed the strange happenings, and meeting Bartholomew for that bonus. Several sinister forces were moving behind the scenes in Vesuva: gangsters, necromancers, greedy companies. The party had made a splashy entrance—but would you survive long enough to make an exit?</p>

  <details class="panel">
    <summary>Into the Pit</summary>
    <div class="panel-body">
  <p>After a long day of stealing from people, conning shopkeepers, singing country music, and killing necromancers, it was time for a moment of calm. The Candleworn Saloon was a bustling place in the evenings, full of clinking glasses and boisterous conversation, but your party managed to find a quiet table in the back.</p>

  <p>Elliot wanted to examine the mysterious medallion he’d palmed from the general store, and the rest of the group wanted to talk about Andre and the dark dealings within the silver mine. The stolen necklace was an ominous piece of jewelry: a simple chain with a jet-black circle of obsidian at its center. The magically inclined members of your party knew it was bad news—but as a group, you were no strangers to bad decisions.</p>

  <p>Several of you tried it on, only to be rejected by an ethereal force that deemed each wearer “not worthy.” Whatever the enchantment was waiting for, it wasn’t you. With that unsettling discovery set aside, your attention turned to Caldera’s mining operation and preparations for the night ahead.</p>

  <p>The bar was, as always, a powerful source of information. Few people are more willing to talk than disgruntled employees after a couple of strong drinks. Tenzoe approached an older, heavily scarred half-orc who seemed to be the de facto leader of the hard-drinking mine workers. The man was gruff and tried to brush him off, but a lifetime of working crowds and charming comrades had taught Andre how to handle working-class roughnecks.</p>

  <p>All it took was some genuine interest, a charming smile (and maybe a little magical mental manipulation). It wasn’t long before the burly mine boss was talking about the dangers of “Shaft 7,” where workers went in… and never went out. He told the party about the vicious foreman, crossbow-toting security, and caustic chemical runoff that ran along the tunnels. Beneath the tough exterior, it was obvious—he was terrified of whatever was happening in the bowels of the dig.</p>

  <p>Elliot and Andre wanted more than rumors. They wanted information kept behind closed doors. The Caldera Mining Company had an office in town, and it was likely empty at this hour. For experienced prowlers, it was child’s play: shimmy up the back of the building, carefully disarm the roof trapdoor, and rifle through the documents in the manager’s office.</p>

  <p>They were hoping for maps and security layouts, but the building was used for clerical and administrative work, not excavation planning. They didn’t leave empty-handed, though—and what they found was disturbing. It was a record of people sent to Shaft 7, with explicit instructions to send more as quickly as possible. Coded, cagey language hinted at something found in the mines—something more dangerous and valuable than ore. Something ancient and powerful, something that could alter the future of the Dust Plains itself.</p>

  <p>Tenzoe and Aezos had a plan of their own—one considerably less subtle. While the rest of the group infiltrated the mines disguised as workers, they would “distract” the guards. Most of the party didn’t realize just how many bodies that distraction would leave behind. With Aezos in position, hiding among the crates hauled up to the entrance, the warlock gathered a trio of foremen around him.</p>

  <p>With a manic shout, Tenzoe unleashed his new arcane powers. Tentacles of necrotic energy burst from the ground, crushing limbs and slashing flesh. The Warren Kings posted as guards tried to intervene, only to be met with scorching rays of fire bursting from Aezos’s hand. It was a wild scene of magic and melee that sent ordinary people scurrying for safety.</p>

  <p>One guard tried to escape—mounting a horse to ride for reinforcements. An eldritch blast tore his head from his shoulders before he made it a dozen paces. The yawning entrance of the mine fell still, with only the sorcerer and warlock left standing in a field of corpses. In retrospect, this made sneaking in somewhat pointless. Still, the destructive pair followed their comrades into the cavernous tunnels.</p>

  <p>The deeper the party went, the more uneasy you became. The walls seemed uncomfortably alive, shifting imperceptibly as you walked down the dimly lit mineshaft. Troughs of caustic chemicals left an oily residue along the stone—it was like walking down the esophagus of a slumbering being of impossible size. At last, you reached the dreaded Seventh Shaft, where a supervisor and a pair of haregon gangsters stood guard.</p>

  <p>Ezit thought fast—she was haregon, and for all they knew, she was just another goon delivering doomed souls to the belly of the beast. She told the checkpoint these were the troublemakers responsible for all the commotion topside: captured and slated to be disposed of. With a slow nod, the group was let through.</p>

  <p>Soon, the rough walls of the cave gave way to polished stone. This was no longer a mine—it was a temple, buried in the rock. There were impediments, wards meant to turn away nonbelievers, but Ezit proved she was quick-witted as well as quick on her feet. The dark staircase and the offering urns were quickly puzzled out.</p>

  <p>Each of you made an offering of words to enter the central chamber: mothers, homes, loves, your past.</p>

  <p>A ritual was underway in the main antechamber. Robed, hooded figures chanted between the sandstone pillars. Aisha moved to conceal herself. Aezos tried to creep into one of the eastern rooms, both searching for an advantage. But Andre had other ideas. He had been kidnapped, beaten—almost sold for parts because of these foul magics. Something in his angelic blood refused to accept the insult. He strode straight up to the figure leading the unholy chorus, ready to bring his fist down on the root of this evil.</p>

  <p>Before a single blow could land, the man drew a curved dagger.</p>

  <p>The blade cut deeply and fatally, but it wasn’t aimed at Andre. The cultist slashed his own throat, pouring his lifeblood onto the cold, unforgiving stone. All around you, the other cultists followed suit, slicking the floor with gore that ran toward the center of the ritual circle. With a massive shudder, their sacrifice was accepted. A crimson portal yawned open on the floor, and a massive, clawed hand reached out.</p>

  <p>In a cloud of brimstone-laced smoke, the torso of a gargantuan demon emerged from the hellish gateway. It slammed down a massive fist, sending crimson waves of flame surging toward the party.</p>

  <p>But you weren’t defenseless. You fought tooth and claw—quite literally, as Andre used druidic shapeshifting to take the form of a cave bear and met the beast head-on. Aezos quickly learned his fire was useless and correctly deduced you’d need cold to bring it down. He and Aisha hurled chromatic orbs and knives of ice into the demon’s flesh while Tenzoe dodged its deadly bite.</p>

  <p>And then there was Elliot—unseen and unheard—who landed the final blow. While the monstrosity was distracted, he climbed up to its fearsome visage and drove a dagger straight into its exposed eye. With a terrifying roar, it recoiled and retreated back to Infernus, weakened but far from dead.</p>

  <p>Now the party had only to uncover the mine’s secrets—and escape with them alive.</p>

  <p>
    What lies in the shadowed corners of the buried temple?<br>
    Will the demon—or the Caldera Mining Company—seek revenge?<br>
    What happened to Gia and Finkle?
